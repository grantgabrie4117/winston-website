Introduction

This project analyzes performance statistics from the Bowling Green State University Overwatch varsity team during the Fall 2025 season. The dataset records each players in-game performance across multiple matches, maps, and opponents.

The purpose of this analysis is to: - Explore win rates across different maps. - Examine the relationship between damage and healing for our support players, Reinfardt and Hobo. - Calculate Kill/Death (KD) ratios to identify player consistency, Map Wins and Losses.

Map Win Analysis

map_results <- data %>%
  group_by(Map.Name, Map.Result) %>%
  summarise(Games = n(), .groups = 'drop')

ggplot(map_results, aes(x = Map.Name, y = Games, fill = Map.Result)) +
  geom_bar(stat = "identity", position = "dodge") +
  labs(title = "Wins and Losses per Map", x = "Map Name", y = "Number of Matches") +
  theme_minimal() +
  theme(axis.text.x = element_text(angle = 45, hjust = 1))

This code block analyzes the teams performance across different maps. It groups the data by map name and map result (Win/Loss) and then counts the number of games for each combination. The ggplot function then creates a bar chart visualizing these wins and losses side-by-side for each map, making it easy to see which maps the team performed well on and which were more challenging.

# Relationship Between Damage and Healing

rh <- data %>% filter(Player.Name %in% c("Reinfardt", "Hobo"))

ggplot(rh, aes(x = Damage.Dealt, y = Healing.Done, color = Player.Name)) +
  geom_point(size = 3) +
  geom_smooth(method = "lm", se = FALSE) +
  labs(title = "Damage vs Healing (Reinfardt & Hobo)", x = "Damage Dealt", y = "Healing Done") +
  theme_minimal()

# Correlation
correlation <- cor(rh$Damage.Dealt, rh$Healing.Done)
correlation

This analysis focuses specifically on the support/tank players Reinfardt and Hobo to examine the relationship between their damage and healing. The code first filters for only these two players. Then, it creates a scatter plot with ggplot to map Damage.Dealt (x-axis) against Healing.Done (y-axis), adding a linear regression line (geom_smooth) to show the trend for each player. Finally, it calculates the Pearson correlation coefficient (cor) to quantify the strength and direction of this relationship.

Conclusion

This analysis provided several insights into the BGSU Overwatch teams Fall 2025 season performance.

Map Performance: The Wins and Losses per Map chart highlights that team performance varied significantly by map. Identifying the maps with lower win rates presents a clear opportunity for targeted practice and strategy refinement.

Player Roles and Consistency: The boxplots for Damage Distribution and K/D Ratio help quantify player roles and consistency. We can see which players are the teams primary damage dealers and which players are most efficient at securing eliminations relative to their deaths. The Comparison of Player Eliminations plot further reinforces these findings, showing the general offensive output of each team member.

Support Dynamics: The focused analysis of Reinfardt and Hobo revealed the specific nature of their damage-to-healing relationship. The calculated correlation (printed in the chunk) quantifies this, suggesting how they balance aggressive play with protective duties.

Team-Wide Roles: The final Healing vs.Damage scatter plot for all players clearly visualizes the different roles on the team, distinguishing high-damage players from high-healing players and those who contribute in both categories.

Overall, this analysis successfully identified player strengths, role specializations, and map-specific team performance. These insights can be valuable for coaching staff and players to understand performance patterns and guide future training.
